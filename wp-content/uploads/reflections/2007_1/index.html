The theme of the next issue is Algebra.

We would like to congratulate all participants of the International Mathematical Olympiad 2007 held in Hanoi, Vietnam. We hope that our journal can provide you with excellent materials to prepare for mathematical competitions. Keep solving problems, develop new ones, send us your solutions and articles. When you do, please include the author's name and affiliation so we can properly credit the submission.

Mathematical Reflections intends to fill the editor's perceived need for a publication aimed primarily at high school students and undergraduates interested in mathematics beyond the classroom. Through articles and problems, we seek to expose readers to a variety of interesting topics that are fully accessible to the target audience.

Through the articles, we hope to expand the horizons of high school and undergraduate readers, introducing them to material outside the scope of most classes. For instructors, the articles provide an intriguing opportunity to move away from a structured curriculum, motivate the addressed problem, and guide students through to the invaluable moments of discovery.

Through the problems, we hope to challenge students to develop their creative problem solving and reasoning skills by devising solutions to the proposed questions. Additionally, we invite the submission of solutions, and exceptional ones will be published, hopefully encouraging students to formally write out their work to be showcased in print.

The journal invites submissions from students, instructors, and enthusiasts alike for all sections.

For information about submitting materials for publication in the journal, visit the submissions page.

Mathematical Reflections is edited by Dr. Titu Andreescu at the University of Texas at Dallas. Contact information is available here.
